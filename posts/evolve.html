Writing Life
we were twelve when the future revealed itself
what do you want to be? life asked questions
we could not answer, mere children so naive.

passion and desires we all we knew, our world
so small and round. our mind flitting to dreams
so big that our feet left the ground.

sometimes our mouths ran loose; words came out
we didn't mean. feelings were hurt, mistakes
were made, but nothing that couldn't be mended
by an ardent apology.

look where we've come, from where we began,
with broomsticks for swords and buckets for hats;
we thank the world for all that it did, and promise
that we will give back.

and maybe we've done whatever we could, to
live life to it's best. bones grow older day
after day, and soon we're laid down to rest.
