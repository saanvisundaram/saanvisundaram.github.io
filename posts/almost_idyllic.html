almost idyllic

Published July 10, 2026 •
Writing Life
i. your world
you have roots in three different places
three different cultures and three different lives,
so many memories with so many faces
you've taken these challenges all in stride;
you've found happiness in being by yourself
you've found joy in learning more names
you've wept and cried because it helped
your origins are vast, but you are the same
for life has thrown a lot your way;
but you are here, unwilling to succumb
because for all that anyone might say
you are sure of where you are from
'i am simply a collage all who i admire
origins are not a place, but an inherent desire.'

ii. the world
there are places you are yet to go
people you are yet to meet
and every one of them will know
where you're from, how you came to be
because you are not a list of instructions
you are more than words on a page
you won't be defined as an application
nor will you settle for a resume
no. you are unique to where you are from
where you are now, how high you will climb
you took your first breath, and your journey began
one that has shaped you and weathered you with time
they might see a flimsy fix, perhaps less than fine
but you are almost idyllic, you are truly divine.

afterword
origins are truly perplexing how they never stay the same
for aren't they the ones we rely on? ones that aren't supposed to change?

but life has taught me, in no simple words, that our origins become weeping mess
of knowing and wondering and wishing and praying that we too find our happy end.
